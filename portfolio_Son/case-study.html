Overview

Block Blast Puzzle Mania transforms classic color-matching puzzle mechanics into a highly engaging progression-driven experience built around satisfying chain reactions and strategic board clearing. Developed by Bibota, the game combines intuitive controls, combo-based scoring, power-up systems, and long-term progression loops that keep players engaged across hundreds of increasingly challenging levels.

My scope on the project focused on gameplay architecture, performance optimization, scalable progression systems, and monetization-supporting reward frameworks designed to sustain long-term player retention.

The Technical Challenges

  • Managing memory consumption generated by large quantities of puzzle assets, UI elements, visual effects, and themed content.
  • Maintaining smooth gameplay performance during large-scale block explosions, combo cascades, and level transitions.
  • Designing scalable reward systems capable of supporting multiple monetization paths across Rewarded Ads and In-App Purchases.
  • Creating retention-driven progression layers that extend player engagement beyond the core puzzle gameplay loop.
  • Supporting hundreds of levels while maintaining efficient content delivery and minimal application footprint.

Memory Optimization: Addressables & Asset Streaming

To reduce application startup times and runtime memory overhead, I restructured the content delivery pipeline around Unity Addressables. Puzzle configurations, level data, visual themes, power-up assets, and UI packages were separated into independently managed asset groups.

Gameplay content loads asynchronously when required and is automatically released when no longer needed, significantly reducing memory pressure during extended play sessions.

Additionally, visual assets were consolidated into carefully optimized Texture Atlases, minimizing texture swaps and preserving efficient draw-call batching throughout gameplay. This ensured stable performance across a broad range of mobile hardware while maintaining visual consistency during intensive combo sequences and particle-heavy events.

LiveOps Architecture: Reward & Monetization Systems

To support long-term progression and monetization flexibility, I implemented a modular reward-processing framework capable of handling multiple validation paths.

Level completion, daily missions, combo objectives, and special events generate progression currencies that feed into centralized reward ledgers. The system supports dual unlock conditions, allowing milestone rewards to be claimed either through Rewarded Video advertisements or direct In-App Purchases.

This abstraction layer enables LiveOps teams to configure promotional campaigns, progression events, and monetization experiments without introducing dependencies into core gameplay systems.

Retention Mechanics: Progression & Mission Loops

To reinforce long-term retention, I developed layered progression systems operating alongside the core puzzle experience. Players advance through hundreds of increasingly challenging stages while simultaneously completing daily objectives, earning bonus rewards, and unlocking additional progression milestones.

This separation between puzzle gameplay and meta-progression allows the core mechanics to remain clean and highly optimized while providing players with continuous goals, reward anticipation, and long-term engagement incentives. The resulting structure increases session frequency, progression completion rates, and overall player lifetime value without compromising gameplay clarity.
